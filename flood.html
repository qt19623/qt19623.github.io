Flood prevention in an urban area

The recreational area as drainage system: small-sized reservoirs

Rabalder Parken in Roskilde, Denmark

Rabalder park is a multipurpose facility which normally serves as a skate park and public leisure space. When there is a huge rain, it can store and drain water equivalent to 10 Olympic swimming pools. Without expensive and inefficient construction of large-scale drainage system

The city with permeable spaces

Sponge cities in China

In 2015, the sponge city project began in 16 cities and expanded to further14 cities next year. The project aims to prevent floods by securing space and time to drain rainwater on the ground before it reaches the river and dam. To achieve it, they are working on many measures like

Constructing waterways and green space near an urban area

Create wetlands

Green roofs

Use permeable asphalt

Rainwater contained can be reused. According to the guidelines of the project, 80% of cities will be able to retain and use 70% of rainwater by 2030. This reduces the risk of flood damage, increases water supply in drought, provides green space for leisure to citizens and so on. Green roofs

Multifunctional roofs in Rotterdam

It is estimated that Rotterdam has 18.5km2 of flat roofs. From 2008, Rotterdam encouraged to use of these roofs multifunctional, such as green roofs which grow plants, blue roofs for rainwater storage and drainage, yellow roofs to generate sustainable energy and so on. They are aiming to turn 1km2 of roofs to become multifunctional and expect 15 to 25 years to achieve it. To encourage private owned roofs (not only municipality-owned), they also provide subsidies and advice.

For the building owners who transformed roofs to retain 30ml of rainwater per 1m2 can receive up to $530 per 1000L of water retained.

They also held Rooftop Days to increase the engagement and interest of citizens from 2015. In 2009, 360,000m2 of roofs were transformed and this can retain 9 million L of rainwater which is equivalent to 4 Olympic swimming pools. Water can be used in drought.

https://www.urbangreenbluegrids.com/projects/rotterdam-the-netherlands/ Before the project was implemented, green roofs were tested in Oud Mathenesse near Rotterdam. 25% of roofs that can be used were transformed into green roofs, and annual overflow volume was reduced by 19.5%.

Introduction

Greenhouse gas emission from agricultural sector has grown over time, worsening environmental problem. Research suggests that this is 26% of total greenhouse gas emission. This implies that if we can change our diet in more environmentally friendly way, we may be able to improve environmental problems significantly. This research aims to analyse how diet of people in the UK has changed, and whether this reduced negative impact on environment. The research will particularly focus on meat consumption, as it produces about a half of total agricultural sector emission and has the biggest potential for reduction with high emission per kilogram.

Motivation for change

According to the survey, about a half of population in the UK has attempted to change their diet to improve health or sustainability. This attempt involves activities like eating less meat or following diet with no animal products.
